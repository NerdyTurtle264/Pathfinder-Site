circa 1321

This is where it all began. Most of us were new to the game, myself included. We played for two years in this campaign so it was our longest by far. We started without much clue on how to go about things. We played for a few months like that then did a reboot where people could redesign their characters and we moved to a new area. With two years of gaming there were tons of adventures that shaped the world and many different NPCs that have carried over to other campaigns.

Their Backstories

Rouge: A kitsune from a forest on the West coast of The Wastes. Not much of one for the childbearing culture of her kitsune village she set off to see the world. She ends up finding more than she bargained for with finding her soul mate and her adopted daughter.

Lilith: A swordmage with a sordid family ancestry. Her great grandfather is one of the great demons of hell. A balor named Eertu. Lilith has a penchant for shiny things. She however isn't good at stealth and as such is frequently foiled by her friends in her pursuit of the shiny.

Castiel: He was a... special paladin. He was devoted to his god but very forgetful. He wouldn't catch certain queues and end up losing his powers. Whenever this would happen he would have to do something to prove himself to his god in order to gain his favor again.

Thomas: Raised by a family of carpenters he set off to find glory for himself. Thomas didn't have the best of luck in his travels however. He often ended up on the nasty end of a trap and in one adventure was cursed with lycanthropy.

Bearington: Raised by an excentric wizard who gifted him with greater intelligence. While growing up he developed a fascination the banjo. Eventually his wizard friend dissapeared and Bearington set out to try to find him once again. He wanted to eventually be knighted so he could be called Sir Bearington.

Benevolence: The lost daughter of the masterful Dover blacksmiths. She got lost long ago and has been searching for them ever since. Ben was a crafty fighter, often resorting to dirty tricks to blink her enemies. Not that she needed to with her luck.

Jen: Started off in a group opposing the party working for one of the anti-heroes of the campaign and when it was revealed what he was doing her group joined with the players as a backup group. She was instrumental in the final battle and without her everything would have been lost

Our Story

Midnight Mirror

It all begins in the small town of Sandpoint. It is a border town between the country of Styrk to the North and "the wastes" to the South. Our group is a guild by the name of The Vaguely Helpful Group hereafter refered to as TVHG. They get a mission from a lower noblewoman to check on her daughter in the nearby town of karpad. The daughter married the baron of the town and had a child recently. In the past month though the town had stopped all contact with the outside world and wouldn't allow anyone past the walls. It was to be a mission to simply make contact with the daughter and report back.

As TVHG gets there the sun is setting and the town is surrounded by walls and bathed in the light of torches and bonfires. The guards at the main entrance don't want to let them in to the town but TVHG convinces the guards to let them past as they have business with the baron.

In the town people are terrified. The townspeople are carrying torches everywhere they go and there are bonfires on every street corner. Through a bit of investigation they find out that people are going missing and getting sick with goiter like protusions that hatch shadow creaturs, killing the host and anyone nearby. The town is made up of the usual races: humans, elves, dwarves, etc. but it also has a population of people that are called fetchlings. These people have ties to the plane of shadow in their ancestry and as such they are getting blamed for the murders that are going on.

The next day they find a mob in the town center shouting. There is a fetchling family being attacked. The group puts an end to it by convincing the mob to disperse. They then foil the cause of people dissapearing. Turns out that some have turned to a fey creature of light to try to combat the shadows. The creature in question is named Ilquis, he has been kidnapping people as sacrifices to bring more of his kind to the plane. He ends up escaping before the group can kill him though.

The group discovers that the manor has an extradimensional prison in the basement. It is a mirror portal to a shadow plane. The mirror is cracked and letting shaddow essence through which is what is infecting people. They go into the prison to try to find a way to stop what is going on. Turns out that the baron had used it to imprison his older brothers so he would get the throne upon the death of his father. One of the brothers ended up fathering a child with a shae woman before he died. They end up finding the heart of the prison. A shadowy tree called a Tendriculos. Using a shard from the broken portal they are able to slay the creature. As it dies the dimension begins to colapse. Because the fabric of this dimension is deteriorating the rooms have strange random effects. Floors turning to quicksand, no gravity, complete impenetrable darkness, and the like.

When they make it out one of the prisoners, Nicasor tries to kill the baron but is convinced to let due process take place and the son of the brother could take over the town when he became old enough.

The Suntingtons

Rodney at the start was playing a fetchling ninja character named Inva Suntington III. Inva's namesake, her grandmother, was a famous elven wizard adventurer hundreds of years ago. Inva got a letter from her senile grandfather saying that her grandmother had died. As TVHG was packing to head to the funeral they heard townspeople on the street screaming. Running out they see a skeletal dragon with a rider atop it in the town square. The rest of the group readies to battle the menace but Inva recognizes it as the family transport "Krunk" and the rider on top as the family's chaufer "Snarl" and tells the group as much to their confusion. After a bit more explaining everyone climbs on Krunk for the journey to the Suntington Manor.

Upon ariving they begin to realize the grandeur of their situation. The manor is massive with extensions in all directions. The group is welcomed in by Valkar the butler. In the parlor they meet Inva's family: Her cousins Olivia and Percival (half-elves) and then her grandfather Theodore who is a... Lich?! In his 500 years of lich-dom his brain has deteriorated and he is now senile. During the reading of the will Inva ends up inheriting the mansion (Theodore gets the guest house) as well as a Headband to increase intelligence. Percival dislikes this since he didn't get anything and during the night he sneaks into the study where a large glass orb is that controls the house and uses it to try to teleport the house away from Inva. The teleportation goes poorly of course and the house gets torn apart. The missing wings of the mansion are spread around in The Wastes. TVHG is faced with an interesting problem though. In it's incomplete state the mansion is unable to further teleport. Theodore in his old man ramblings says something about "get the house to fly like the Old City. Oh what was it's name...(walks over to a shelf and picks up a compass, reading it says) Ulduvai". The compass he picked up is a relic from a crashed trade city. The compass is supposed to always point towards the city. Maybe they could salvage parts of the city to make the mansion fly to the missing parts.

Crucible of Chaos

Exploring the new area around the mansion they discover they are now in the middle of a desert. To the west is a pirate city called Kaer Maga built at the mouth of a river. While exploring they get in some trouble when Thomas catches a pickpocket trying to rob him. He takes the would be thief to the "guard" who are so corrupt that they refuse to take action since the pickpocket paid protection money to Captain Kurth, one of the High Captains of the city. Out of character, upon finding out that the guards won't do anything about the pickpocket, Mike says that he strips the pickpocket and steals his clothes. Mind you they never left the guard station. He is doing this in front of the guards (which are corrupt yes, but they have been paid off by the high captain, not by Mike's character Thomas). He is then chased off by the guards and the group has to flee, following the compass to the North.

They travel for many days reaching a mountain range. The compass leads them through a mountain pass to a valley between the mountains. They see the city at the end of the valley. Great golden walls surround it. As they get to the gates there are demons guarding it who attack them as they approach. The fight doesn't isn't going well and as the group is almost beaten they hear voices behind them. Turning around they see another adventuring party. The party is made up of two fetchlings, a human, an aasimar, and a monkey with an Allosaurus companion. One of the fetchlings taps the human fighter on the back launching him dozens of feet, landing next to one of the demons where he smashes its head in. Our party, thankful for the help of this new group, decides to camp outside the gate for the night and invites the new group to join them. During the night they discover that the group is named the Knight Lights and the fetchling that performed the magic earlier is named Jarlaxle. Jarlaxle hired the Knight Lights to venture with him to discover the lost city of Ulduvai. Inva of course finds Jarlaxle attractive so she sneaks into his tent in the middle of the night. When morning comes she finds him gone along with her headband.

The gate is still locked so the group climbs the walls. Once inside though they discover why the city crashed in the first place. There is chaotic energy radiating through the city causing things like immortal parrots, floating globes of water with fish swimming in it, and stone walls occasionally ripple like a flag in the wind. Exploring the city they discover the source of all the chaos is a huge stone pillar. As they are trying to figure out what to do about it they hear a noise from between a couple buildings. A huge amorphous creature is coming towards them. It turns out that the stone had summoned a creature of pure chaos, a Shoggoth. Hundreds of years ago the people of the city heard about a great evil in the land. Fearing this evil they turned to the stone for power against the evil. The stone warped the city and it's people in the process and summoned the shoggoth which brought the city down. After they defeat the shoggoth the stone is weakened enough that they are able to destroy it with a dispell magic spell. The stone crumbles and they hear clapping and cheering from a bluff overlooking the battlefield. It is Jarlaxle and the Knight Lights. TVHG is badly injured from their fight with the creature so they aren't much up for fighting to get the headband back. They use persuasion to convince the Knight Lights that their employer is up to no good. When the Knight Lights begin to turn on Jarlaxle he escapes by using a magic item to create a tunnel below himself and drop down.

The chaos of the stone created rifts that pulled members of the old city in. From the crumbled remains of the stone stand some of the original member of the ruling council of the city. They begin surveying the city and realize that it is still in working order but some of the magic items that allow the city to work are missing. Turns out that the shoggoth made everyone fled and kept looter from destroying the city. The first item is a metal rod that can capture and store lightning bolts, this was the original protection and power for the city. The second item they will need to find to get the city to work again is a bag that has a portal to the plane of wind, this provides the lift for the city.

Canidae Copse

Since the group didn't have any leads on where to find these items they had nothing really to do till then. Rouge decided that now would be a good time to officially adopt Bearington. TVHG travel to her village on the West coast. Both the village and forest it is in are named Canidae Copse. Some things to know about Kitsune. First they are an all female race. Males born from a kitsune are always the fathers race and females are always the mothers. Second they have a huge focus on childbearing in their culture. Third they live in family units and non kitsune in the community are rare. Because of the second and third points males coming to the village get a lot of attention from the kitsune women. During the night there are stories told around the campfire to scare the children. One of them is about the headless armor. It appears in towns and the townsfolk dissapear.

When the group gets there it happens to coincide with a festival so the group expects to find merriment but instead there is a lot of tension and hostility among the hostility. During the talks with the Matron mother about adoption she says that new comers to the community have to prove themselves to be accepted. The task she has in mind is that the forest they live in has become tainted as of late. She asks them to remove it.

As the group ventures into the forest they notice that some of the trees are warped and blackened confirming the matrons suspicions. There are small creatures called Leshys tending to these trees trying to stop the spread of infection. Up ahead they see large figures flitting about between the trees in the distance. Winged abominations that swoop down and attack the party. Venturing further one of the trees attacks as they walk beneath it's boughs. As they come to the center of the infection they see more of the winged creatures up ahead and one that is larger than the rest that seems to be controlling the rest. During the ensuing battle they see another figure appear. It is a large tree spirit that the locals refer to as the goddess of the forest. She helps by restoring the party during the fight.

Cantankerous Captain Kurth

The group had heard of a legendary pirate from Kaer Maga who has control of the wind. Entering the city they begin trying to gather information. They hear that Captain Kurth has been one of the high captains for over 200 years despite being only human. He has a manor in the town but never sets foot in the town. While they are in one of the taverns gathering information there is a raucous dwarf in a corner boasting about how he is going to kill Captain Kurth but he hates boats. The group sits with him and begins asking him about why he is going to do so. He responds that he was hired by someone and then gets distracted and seems to be talking to an imaginary figure. The dwarf says "Oh he says if you want you can take over the contract to kill the ship rat. Here is a boat to get out to sea." and procedes to toss them a long shallow wooden box. The group understandably looks at him like he is crazy. The dwarf then says "Oh and if you actually do it he says there is an added bonus for you". The group equal parts confused and intrigued asks the dwarf his name. "I am the great Athrogate! Bounty hunter and murderer for money extraordinaire. I am sure you have heard of me." After the run in with Athrogate they head to the docks. Figuring it is just a joke they throw the wooden box into the water. To their surprise it begins expanding into an actual boat big enough for a dozen people. Maybe the dwarf wasn't so crazy afterall.

TVHG spent a couple days at sea before seeing their intended target in the distance. Kurths ship has heading directly up wind at them. When it got close enough they realized their miscalculation. The pirate ship was way larger than their own and they had no offensive ranged weapons besides Liliths fireballs. Kurths ship pulled up to theirs broadside to show the cannons and balistas and a voice began shouting out demanding them to drop their weapons. Without any way to counterattack or prevent a cannonball from sinking them the group did as they were told. As the ship moved closer to send people down Rouge shrank down to her fox form and had Thomas throw her onto the ship. From there she began trying to take out the people manning the weapons. The rest of the group in the confusion began making their way up the boarding ladder to the top deck as well. As they take out the crew on the deck it becomes apparent that they haven't seen or fought the captain. knocking on the captains cabin they hear him inside "I told you not to bother me Jack. How do you expect me to have you replace me when I retire if you can't handle that dinky ship." Turns out that Captain Kurth is just a title passed down from one person to another. That is how it has survived for so long. When the group finally gets him out of the cabin he is drunk off his ass and doesn't put up much of a fight. They sail back to Kaer Maga for the reward from Athrogate.

When they get back to the city and are walking to the bar they first met Athrogate in they see a few of the buildings up ahead have colapsed. Getting closer there are hunks of green crystal in the center of the destruction. The townspeople are avoiding getting close to the area like they are afraid of it. When asked they just say that the building there was blown apart as a green tower was raised in it's place and maybe half an hour after it appeared it crumbled.

Upon entering the bar they see the dwarf in the corner where he was before but there is an odd figure sitting with him. Before they have a chance to hail the dwarf the figure turns around like he expected them to come in the door. "Welcome my friends!" He then says to the rest of the room "These are the ones who captured Captain Kurth"

Summersdale

One of TVHG had heard of the city of Summersdale not too far off that always has a lightning storm on top of it. They head off to try to figure out if it is related to the item that powers the city. As they approach the city the storm on top of it is looming and the sound is deafening. As they make their way through the streets the townspeople flee inside their houses at the sight of them. The lightning bolts from the storm are only striking one building in the center of town, it is almost constant strikes. By the time they make their way to the city center they see an armed group gathered there awaiting their approach. The mob of people is made up of townspeople for the inner portions and clergy members on the outside to defend them. A woman is standing in the front of the group, clad in black iron armor with a matching scythe she is spinning in her hands. Few things about this city. First it is built on one of the largest Cold Iron mines in the world. Second about a hundred years ago a demon by the name of Eertu Balor attacked the city in order to stop the production of cold iron as it is particurlarly effective against his kind. He was stopped but the rift that he opened in the earth for his invasion is still there letting smaller demons through. It has come to be known as the World Wound. Third the city is run by the church which protects against the demons. The figure head of the church is called the Arbiter. They are outfitted in the best armor and given a holy weapon. This woman in the front is the Arbiter. The townspeople see the group and immediately notice that Lilith has demon ancestry. Hence the less than warm welcome. Fourth there has started to be sightings of undead in town.

After some bravado-ing around the group has found out that the Arbiter is named Holly Bredove. Lilith had to lie of course about her demon ancestry since it was her great grandfather who caused the rift in the first place. It is also discovered that the reason for the lighting striking the chapel tower is the lightning binder is up there. The power from the lightning binder is being channeled into another magical item, the Thread of Life. The thread is being used to bind the World Wound. Removing the Lightning Binder would leave the thread underpowered and unable to bind the wound anymore, releasing the full force of the demons. The group ends up staying in the town a few nights to try to figure out a solution.

During the night a member of the group is in the graveyard to try to figure out what is going on and ends up spotting Holly on her patrol during the night. As they are following her she stops at one of the graves and pulls out what turns out to be the Life Thread. She uses it to raise the corpse. As it stands up she slays it and the group member confronts her about it. It is discovered that she wants to resurrect her dead husband. He was one of the Arbiters before her. She believes that with enough power she can bring him back. She has been testing the thread and how much would be needed to bring someone back completely. If she used the full power of the thread though it would completely open the wound though.

The group offers their services as well as the services of their allies to help secure the rift once it is open. With multiple Arbiters and the extra forces it is believed that they should be able to close it completely. Rouge recruits her clan, Thomas and Castiel recruit their villages, Benevolence recruits her grandfather as well as Kronk and Snarl. Holly sets up in the church to do the resurrection and the rest of the party is at the rift ready for what is to come out. As the rift expands more and more powerful demons make their way through. After fighting off wave after wave of them they can feel something bigger clawing it's way through. What emerges is a large winged demon wielding a burning whip in one clawed hand and a sword in the other. Krunk and Snarl move to fight it but as they get in range the demon with astonishing quickness side steps and beheads Krunk. Lilith in her heart recognizes this demon for what it is, who it is. It is her great grandfather Eertu Balor, the Demon that opened the portal in the first place. He moves towards the party with pounding steps and begins to address the group but stops short smelling something in the air... His progeny. Turning to Lilith he asks her "Why do you raise a sword against me and my forces? You belong by my side slaughtering this filth. You are of my own brood."

This naturally surprises the rest of the group as they didn't know anything about Liliths ancestry since she had kept it as a closely guarded secret. Lilith responds with "I cannot abide by what is going on and will not allow any harm to come to the town or her friends" Eertu gives a hearty laugh at this and begins his assault. Part way through Holly and Lucen make their way to the battlefield and throw cold iron weapons to the group and begin taking out their decades of frustration for the sadness and pain that they suffered at the hands of his kind. Upon his death Eertu's body begins to burn. Balors when slain on the material plane return to their own plane in a burst of flame. So that happens, almost taking some of the party members lower on health with it.

With their balor general vanquished the rest of the demons ranks scatter or are cut down. Before long though it is the time to take stock of the loss of the day. Many former mortals are strewn about the battlefield. Theodore Suntington moves to his beheaded mount Krunk. Long ago it was he that brought him to be so it isn't much to reconstruct him again. The victory is bittersweet as the city may lose a few dozen a year to the demons but they have just lost hundreds to quell it. None the less they are thankful for the aid of the group as it will soon mean the end of the threat.

The Lightning Binder and Life thread are taken from their places in the chapel and brought to the field. The thread has shortened considerably in the resurrection of Lucen Bredove. The thread is then woven through the edges of the rift, barely making it the whole way around before running out of thread. The end of the thread is tied to the Lightning Binder and it is levitated for the storm to recharge. A great lightning bolt comes down striking the binder. The thread begins cinching the rift closed while the lightning sears the edges like cauterizing a wound. After a couple more lightning strikes the World Wound is closed and all that is left is a furrow of ground and scorched earth. Lilith as thanks for her help in the battle is given the sword that originally drove Eertu back hundreds of years ago. Balor Bane. With the rift closed the lightning binder is no longer needed by Summersdale. TVHG takes it back to Ulduvai.
